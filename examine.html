Where questions meet evidence.

Examine mirrors the process of disciplinary investigation: generating questions, building knowledge, analyzing evidence, and arriving at a defensible, evidence-based position.

💡 The Teacher Mindset

You're not delivering content — you're building a case. Design instructional cycles that push students to connect disciplinary knowledge to the question of what causes this challenge and what could actually fix it.

Each standard you teach should answer a question students already have about root causes or solutions.

By the End
Success Criteria

By the end of Examine, students should demonstrate growth in three areas of disciplinary reasoning and evidence use:

1

Apply Disciplinary Concepts

Accurately apply targeted content standards to explain how the system behind the challenge operates and what causes the problem.

NGSS SEP 2, SEP 6 · CCSS R.1, R.4, W.2 · C3 D2
2

Identify & Analyze Root Causes

Use disciplinary evidence to identify underlying causes, analyze impact and magnitude, and compare competing explanations.

NGSS SEP 4, SEP 6 · CCSS W.1 · C3 D3
3

Evaluate & Justify Solutions

Compare solution pathways using criteria and evidence, then defend a solution direction by synthesizing findings across all instructional cycles.

NGSS SEP 7, ETS1-2 · CCSS W.1, W.9, SL.4 · C3 D4.6

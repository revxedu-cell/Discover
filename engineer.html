⚙️ Phase 3 of DEEDS

Where ideas become real.

Scholars take on a professional role to design, plan, build, and refine a solution that directly addresses a root cause of the challenge — and demonstrate that it is ready for real-world use.

By the End
Success Criteria

By the end of Engineer, students should demonstrate growth across three core areas of design and professional construction:

1

Design the Solution

Make clear design choices that address a specific root cause, meet defined criteria and constraints, and can be justified with evidence and reasoning.

NGSS ETS1-1, ETS1-2 · CCSS W.2, MP.4 · C3 D4.7
2

Plan & Build

Develop a logical construction sequence, use professional tools and disciplinary methods safely and effectively, and follow the plan while adapting as needed.

NGSS SEP 3, ETS1-3 · CCSS W.4 · C3 D4
3

Test, Revise & Demonstrate Readiness

Test the solution during construction, revise based on feedback and evidence, and demonstrate that it functions as intended and is ready for enactment.

NGSS ETS1-3 · CCSS W.5, SL.4 · C3 D4.6

💡 The Teacher Mindset

You're not the expert builder — you're the project manager. Create conditions for students to make real design decisions, use professional tools responsibly, and take genuine pride in what they create.

The goal isn't a perfect product. It's a functional solution that students can defend and are ready to put into action.

🤝 Community Partners

During Engineer, community partners act as professional mentors, advisors, and end-user reviewers who help students build and refine with real-world standards in mind. Partners may engage in several ways:

🏗️
Professional mentors in the relevant fieldguiding students in using tools, materials, and disciplinary methods safely and effectively.
📋
Practitioners who review design plansproviding feedback on feasibility, alignment to criteria, and real-world constraints before building begins.
👥
End users or community memberstesting prototypes and providing authentic feedback on whether the solution works for the people it's meant to serve.
🏢
Partner organizationssupplying materials, workspace, or professional context that makes the build more authentic and consequential.

Partner involvement during Engineer raises the stakes for students — building something that a real professional will review changes how carefully students plan and how seriously they take revision.

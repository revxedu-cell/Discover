ENGINEER Daily Routine
⏱ 5–10 min
Production Plan
At the start of every build session, set your team up for focused, effective work by checking in on three things.
✏️ Individual⏳ 2–3 min
✅
Check Criteria & Constraints
Review what your solution needs to do and what limits you're working within.
How well are we meeting our criteria?
Which criteria or constraints do we most need to focus on today?
›
✏️ Individual⏳ 2–3 min
🎯
Set Goals & Make a Plan
Decide what you'll accomplish today and who does what. Log your goal in your Daily Log.
What is our best next step?
What do we want to get done by the end of today?
Who will do what?
›
✏️ Individual⏳ 1–3 min
🤝
Process Check
Briefly check in on how your team is working together before diving in.
What's working well on our team?
What might we need to adjust?
What do we need from each other today?
Teacher Background — Engineer
Production Plan
When to use
Daily, at the start of every build session
Standards Alignment
NGSS SEP 6
Constructing explanations and designing solutions — daily planning against criteria mirrors the iterative design process in authentic engineering.
CCSS W.5
Production and distribution of writing — students develop and strengthen planning through reflection and revision of their daily logs over time.
C3 D4
Taking informed action — students translate criteria knowledge into actionable, coordinated work steps each session.
Math MP.6
Attend to precision — the criteria check develops habits of precision in materials, measurements, and fit-to-standard.
Facilitation Notes
  • Protect this time — 5–10 minutes of upfront planning consistently outperforms unstructured build starts. Announce and enforce it as a non-negotiable.
  • Circulate during the Production Plan and use what you hear to target your coaching during the build session. Teams who set vague goals need tighter accountability.
  • Reference the criteria explicitly when teams check in. Prompt: "Show me in your criteria which one you're working toward today."
  • For younger scholars, provide a simple Production Plan card at each table with the three prompts printed. They can write directly on it each day.
  • At the end of sessions, invite one or two teams to share what they completed. This public accountability reinforces the planning habit.
Why this, why now
Ownership: Students set their own daily goals rather than following a teacher-issued task list. This builds executive function and professional self-management.

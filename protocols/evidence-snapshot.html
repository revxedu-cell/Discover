Teacher Background — Do
Evidence Snapshot
When to use
End of each implementation session
Standards Alignment
NGSS SEP 3
Planning and carrying out investigations — daily observation during implementation is an authentic scientific practice; Evidence Snapshot builds that habit.
CCSS SL.1
Collaborative discussion — the Turn & Talk format requires students to articulate evidence and share observations with precision in dialogue.
C3 D3.3
Gathering and evaluating sources — students prioritize and evaluate the significance of individual pieces of evidence relative to their question.
Math SMP.6
Attend to precision — naming evidence precisely (what, where, when observed) builds mathematical and scientific precision habits.
Facilitation Notes
  • Keep it genuinely quick — 3 minutes is enough if students know the prompts. Post them visibly in the room during the Do phase so no time is lost re-reading them each day.
  • Call on 2–3 pairs to share their "surprise" each day. This builds a class-wide observation log that can be referenced during the full Data Dive.
  • If students consistently have "no surprises," probe further: "What went exactly as expected? How do you know your prediction was right?"
  • For extended implementation periods (multi-week projects), brief weekly Data Dives can be anchored by accumulating Evidence Snapshot notes from the daily routine.
  • The Next Steps prompt directly feeds session planning. Consider collecting these briefly and using them to shape the next session's structure or focus.
Why this, why now
Ownership: Students select their own "most important evidence" — this requires judgment, not recall. It treats scholars as active evaluators of their own data, not passive recorders.

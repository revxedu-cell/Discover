🚀 Phase 4 of DEEDS

Where solutions meet reality.

Scholars put their solution into action in the real world, collect evidence of what happens, and use data to honestly evaluate impact, effectiveness, and what comes next.

By the End
Success Criteria

By the end of Do, students should demonstrate growth across three areas of real-world implementation and evidence-based evaluation:

1

Enact a Solution

Implement the solution with real users, systems, or environments; document the process; and adapt when real-world conditions require it.

NGSS ETS1-3 · CCSS W.2, SL.4 · C3 D4.7
2

Collect & Use Evidence

Gather relevant data about impact and effectiveness using appropriate tools — measurements, observations, surveys — and organize findings clearly.

NGSS SEP 4, SEP 5 · CCSS W.7 · MP.4
3

Evaluate Impact & Effectiveness

Use evidence to explain what worked, what didn't, and why — identifying limitations, tradeoffs, unintended consequences, and meaningful next steps.

NGSS SEP 6, SEP 7 · CCSS W.1, W.9 · C3 D4.6

💡 The Teacher Mindset

Imperfect results are expected — and that's the point. Design a safe-to-fail environment where students can put their ideas to the test and use evidence to honestly evaluate what actually happened.

The goal isn't a flawless outcome. It's building the habit of learning from evidence — which is what real changemakers do.

🤝 Community Partners

During Do, community partners are the real-world recipients and evaluators of the solution. Their engagement makes the implementation authentic and the evidence meaningful. Partners may engage in several ways:

👥
Real users of the solutionwho interact with what students built and provide honest feedback on whether it actually helps.
🏢
Partner organizationswho host or facilitate the implementation, giving students access to a real context and real conditions.
📊
Data collaboratorswho help students understand what evidence to collect, how to collect it honestly, and what it actually means.
🔬
Practitioners or advisorswho help students interpret unexpected outcomes and connect their findings to real-world standards of effectiveness.

Authentic partner feedback during Do is what separates evidence-based evaluation from guessing. It pushes students to engage honestly with results rather than just describing what they hoped would happen.

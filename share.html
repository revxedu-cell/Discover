📢 Phase 5 of DEEDS

Where learning goes public.

Scholars make their learning visible — explaining what they investigated, what they built, and what they changed, to a real audience beyond the classroom. Share is a moment of visibility, accountability, and celebration.

💡 The Teacher Mindset

This is not a performance — it's a conversation. Design a Share experience where students practice explaining their thinking, not just presenting it, and engage authentically with the people their work was meant to serve.

Visibility and accountability are built throughout the project. Share is when students experience what it means to stand behind their work.

By the End
Success Criteria

By the end of Share, students should demonstrate growth in three areas of public communication and reflection:

1

Communicate Learning

Explain what was learned and done clearly to an audience outside the classroom, referencing evidence from across the full project to support explanations.

CCSS SL.4–6 · NGSS SEP 6, SEP 8 · C3 D4.3
2

Reflect on Growth

Reflect on how thinking, skills, and sense of self developed across the project — using specific examples to explain what changed and why it matters.

CCSS SL.1, SL.4 · NGSS SEP 7
3

Engage with Your Audience

Respond thoughtfully to questions, critique, or feedback — clarifying, defending, or building on ideas when challenged by audience members.

CCSS SL.1, SL.3 · C3 D4.6-8 · NGSS SEP 6

💡 The Teacher Mindset

You're not teaching content — you're curating wonder. Design moments that make scholars lean in and say, "Wait, why is this happening?"

Think documentary filmmaker: make the challenge impossible to ignore.

🤝 Community Partners

During the Discover phase, community partners help students understand the real-world challenge from multiple perspectives. Partners may engage in several ways:

👥
Clients or community members experiencing the challenge firsthandhelping students understand the human impact of the issue.
🏢
Organizations already working on the challengesharing what actions are being taken and where needs still exist.
🔬
Professionals or expertsoffering technical knowledge or industry perspectives that deepen students' understanding.
📍
Field sites or community locationswhere students can visit, observe the challenge in context, and ask questions directly.

Together, these experiences help students ground the challenge in real life and begin identifying the questions they need to investigate next.

Grades K–2: Peaceful Paws

"How can we create calming toys that help pets feel calm while they wait to be adopted?"

Students learn that shelter dogs often feel anxious, which can prevent them from getting adopted. Over 5 sessions, they experience the challenge firsthand, examine evidence, and make a case for why solving it matters.
